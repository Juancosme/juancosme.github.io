Research Projects

Broadband Infrastructure and Local Economic Outcomes

This project evaluates the impact of USDA ReConnect broadband investment on local labor markets using a difference-in-differences and event study framework across U.S. counties.

The analysis combines USDA ReConnect program data with county-level employment, income, and rural classification data to examine whether broadband infrastructure investment produces measurable short-run effects on employment and household income.

Methods: Difference-in-Differences, Event Study, Panel Data

College Closures and Local Labor Markets

This project analyzes how college closures affect local labor market outcomes using a difference-in-differences framework with exposure-based variation across counties.

The study examines whether areas more exposed to college closures experience stronger employment effects, highlighting the role of local institutions as economic anchors in regional labor markets.

Methods: Difference-in-Differences, Exposure-Based Analysis, Panel Data

Earned Income Tax Credit and Labor Supply (Replication)

This presentation analyzes how the Earned Income Tax Credit affects labor force participation, based on an existing empirical study.
